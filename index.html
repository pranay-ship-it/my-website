Chapter Two

Undergraduate: IIIT Delhi & Leadership

I studied Computer Science at IIIT Delhi, but for me, undergraduate life was all about what happened beyond the classroom. This was the chapter where I discovered leadership, truly discovered it. It started with learning to be on stage, finding my voice in front of a crowd. Then came managing a team of 5, followed by an exceptionally capable team of 30, and eventually, an organisation of 400.

I enjoyed coding and was pretty decent at it. But I liked learning about startups more. When campus placements came around, I opted out entirely. I firmly believed, and told everyone around me, that coding would be automated in the next 15 years, and that only a handful of the very best engineers in the world would still be relevant. I knew I wasn't in the top 0.01% of engineers. So I chose a different path.

Side thought: in this era, having a computer science background is incredibly useful if you want to build a startup.

Chapter Three

Fitzura: My First Startup

Fitzura was my first startup, built with two close friends. The concept was simple but bold: track users' fitness activity, assign them points based on performance, and let them unlock exclusive apparel collections only after hitting certain milestones. Think of it as a battle pass, but for fitness.

People show off their wealth through the brands they wear. We wanted to create a world where they could show off their level of fitness instead. The vision was to partner with brands like Adidas and Nike to launch exclusive collections, positioning Fitzura as the platform that tracks fitness, assigns scores, and unlocks apparel when certain levels are reached.

The first Fitzura collection: "Athlete," "Passion," and "Take Pride in Passion" designs.

Living the Fitzura ethos, competing and pushing fitness limits.
Gaining different interests and running with Adidas Runners club, ways to stay closer to the target market and understand the user's perspective for Fitzura.

We were three computer science students building this in college, passionate about fitness but lacking the fashion industry connections needed to scale. Eventually, we moved on to pursue other opportunities. But the spirit of Fitzura lives on.

Chapter Four

Graduate School: USC Marshall

At USC Marshall School of Business, I continued to pursue entrepreneurship with the same intensity I'd brought to everything before it.

In one of my classes called Venture Lab, when everyone was introducing themselves with their name and one quick investment thesis, I said my name and admitted I didn't have a solid investment thesis yet. But I did share a conviction: in the next 5 to 10 years, the typical SaaS wouldn't be a hot investment thesis anymore, unless it has network effects or something similarly defensible.
